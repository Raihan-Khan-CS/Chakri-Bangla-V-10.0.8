Introduction to Parts of Speech

In English grammar, Parts of Speech are the categories to which words are assigned based on their syntactic function. In simple terms, every word in a sentence has a job to do, and the "Part of Speech" tells us what that job is. Mastering Parts of Speech is the first and most crucial step in understanding English grammar, essential for sentence structure, error correction, and overall language proficiency. বিসিএস পরীক্ষার জন্য Parts of Speech সনাক্ত করা এবং তাদের সঠিক ব্যবহার জানা অপরিহার্য।

Traditionally, there are eight parts of speech in English:

  1. Noun
  2. Pronoun
  3. Adjective
  4. Verb
  5. Adverb
  6. Preposition
  7. Conjunction
  8. Interjection

এই পৃষ্ঠায় আমরা প্রতিটি Parts of Speech এবং তাদের প্রকারভেদ নিয়ে বিস্তারিত আলোচনা করেছি, সাথে রয়েছে অসংখ্য উদাহরণ।

1. Noun (বিশেষ্য)

A Noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea. সোজা কথায়, কোনো কিছুর নামকেই Noun বলে।

Classification of Nouns

Nouns can be broadly classified into two types: Concrete Noun (যাকে দেখা, শোনা, স্পর্শ বা ঘ্রাণ নেওয়া যায়) and Abstract Noun (অনুভব করা যায় কিন্তু ইন্দ্রিয় দ্বারা উপলব্ধি করা যায় না).

Concrete Nouns are further divided into four types:

A. Proper Noun (নামবাচক বিশেষ্য): The specific name of a particular person, place, organization, or thing. It always begins with a capital letter.

Examples:
Persons: Rahim, Karim, Shakespeare, Einstein, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, Stephen Hawking, Marie Curie, Nelson Mandela, Salma, John, David, Emily, Michael Jordan, Isaac Newton.
Places: Dhaka, London, Bangladesh, India, Asia, Pacific Ocean, Padma River, Mount Everest, Sahara Desert, New York, Tokyo, Paris, Rome, Sydney, Cairo, Moscow.
Organizations: Google, Microsoft, United Nations, World Bank, BRAC, Grameen Bank, University of Dhaka, NASA, WHO, UNICEF.
Days & Months: Sunday, Monday, January, February, July.
Holidays: Eid-ul-Fitr, Durga Puja, Christmas, Independence Day.
Books/Movies: Geetanjali, Harry Potter, Titanic, The Quran, The Bible.
Brands: Apple, Samsung, Toyota, Nike, Coca-Cola.

B. Common Noun (জাতিবাচক বিশেষ্য): The general, non-specific name for a class or group of persons, places, or things.

Examples:
Persons: man, woman, boy, girl, student, teacher, doctor, player, writer, mother, father, friend, scientist, engineer, artist, farmer, king, queen, president.
Places: city, country, village, river, mountain, ocean, desert, continent, island, street, building, school, hospital, park, library, restaurant.
Things: book, pen, table, chair, computer, mobile, car, tree, flower, animal, bird, fish, house, ship, train, watch, bag, ball, phone, water bottle, laptop, bicycle.
Sentence Examples: The boy is playing in the park. Dhaka is a big city. My friend gave me a book.

C. Collective Noun (সমষ্টিবাচক বিশেষ্য): A word that refers to a group or collection of people, animals, or things as a single entity.

Examples:
army, navy, police, team, jury, committee, government, parliament, audience, crowd, class, family, staff, group, herd (of cattle), flock (of sheep/birds), swarm (of bees), pack (of wolves), bunch (of keys/grapes), fleet (of ships), library (of books), orchestra, band, choir, panel, board, council, mob, gang, crew, pair, dozen, trio, quartet.
Sentence Examples: The jury gave its verdict. Our cricket team won the match. A flock of birds is flying.

D. Material Noun (বস্তুবাচক বিশেষ্য): The name of a material or substance from which things are made. These are typically uncountable.

Examples:
gold, silver, iron, cotton, wood, glass, plastic, paper, diamond, steel, bronze, copper, cement, clay, sand, water, milk, oil, sugar, salt, rice, wheat, flour, leather, wool, silk, rubber, air, oxygen, coal, petrol, chalk, marble, stone, sunshine, rain.
Sentence Examples: The ring is made of gold. The chair is made of wood. We need water to live.

E. Abstract Noun (গুণবাচক বা ভাববাচক বিশেষ্য): The name of a quality, action, state, or idea that we cannot perceive with our five senses.

Examples:
Qualities: honesty, bravery, kindness, wisdom, beauty, courage, loyalty, integrity, patience, generosity, brilliance, stupidity, cruelty, goodness, evil.
Actions: laughter, movement, judgment, hatred, theft, decision, flight, arrival, departure.
States: childhood, boyhood, youth, slavery, freedom, liberty, poverty, wealth, sickness, health, sleep, death, peace, war.
Ideas/Concepts: love, hate, friendship, patriotism, truth, justice, liberty, democracy, religion, knowledge, information, belief, dream, thought, idea, opinion, theory, memory, culture, education, grammar, physics, mathematics.
Sentence Examples: Honesty is the best policy. We should always speak the truth. Laughter is good medicine.

2. Pronoun (সর্বনাম)

A Pronoun is a word used in place of a noun to avoid repetition. Noun-এর পরিবর্তে যে শব্দ ব্যবহৃত হয়, তাকে Pronoun বলে।

Classification of Pronouns

1. Personal Pronoun (ব্যক্তিবাচক সর্বনাম): Refers to a specific person or thing. (I, we, you, he, she, it, they, me, us, him, her, them).

2. Demonstrative Pronoun (নির্দেশক সর্বনাম): Points to a specific noun. (This, that, these, those).

3. Interrogative Pronoun (প্রশ্নবাচক সর্বনাম): Used to ask questions. (Who, whom, whose, which, what).

4. Relative Pronoun (সাপেক্ষ সর্বনাম): Connects a clause or phrase to a noun or pronoun. (Who, whom, whose, which, that, what).

5. Indefinite Pronoun (অনির্দিষ্ট সর্বনাম): Refers to a non-specific person or thing. (All, any, anyone, anybody, anything, another, both, each, either, everybody, everyone, everything, few, many, most, neither, nobody, none, no one, one, other, several, some, somebody, someone, something).

6. Reflexive Pronoun (আত্মবাচক সর্বনাম): Refers back to the subject of the sentence. (Myself, ourselves, yourself, yourselves, himself, herself, itself, themselves).

7. Emphatic Pronoun (জোরারোপমূলক সর্বনাম): Used to add emphasis to the subject. The words are the same as reflexive pronouns.

8. Distributive Pronoun (বণ্টনমূলক সর্বনাম): Refers to persons or things one at a time. (Each, either, neither, every, everyone).

9. Reciprocal Pronoun (পারস্পরিক সর্বনাম): Expresses a mutual relationship. (Each other, one another).

Sentence Examples:

Personal: He is a good boy. Give the book to me.
Demonstrative: This is my house. Those are beautiful flowers.
Interrogative: Who are you? What do you want?
Relative: The man who came here is my brother.
Indefinite: Someone is at the door. Many were invited.
Reflexive: He hurt himself.
Emphatic: I myself did the work.
Distributive: Each of the boys gets a prize.
Reciprocal: The two brothers love each other.

3. Adjective (বিশেষণ)

An Adjective is a word that describes or modifies a noun or a pronoun. এটি Noun বা Pronoun-এর দোষ, গুণ, অবস্থা, সংখ্যা, পরিমাণ ইত্যাদি প্রকাশ করে।

Classification of Adjectives

1. Adjective of Quality / Descriptive Adjective (গুণবাচক): Shows the kind or quality of a person or thing. (good, bad, beautiful, ugly, intelligent, honest, poor, rich, tall, short, black, white, red, big, small, long, hot, cold).

2. Adjective of Quantity (পরিমাণবাচক): Shows how much of a thing is meant. (some, any, much, little, enough, all, no, whole, sufficient, half).

3. Adjective of Number / Numeral Adjective (সংখ্যাবাচক): Shows how many persons or things are meant. (one, two, three, first, second, third, all, no, many, few, some, any, several, various).

4. Demonstrative Adjective (নির্দেশক): Points out which person or thing is meant. (this, that, these, those, such).

5. Interrogative Adjective (প্রশ্নবাচক): Used with a noun to ask a question. (What, which, whose).

6. Possessive Adjective (সম্বন্ধবাচক): Shows possession or belonging. (my, our, your, his, her, its, their).

Sentence Examples:

Quality: Rahim is an honest man. The sky is blue.
Quantity: I ate some rice. There is little water in the jug.
Number: He has three pens. The first boy in the class is my friend.
Demonstrative: This book is mine. Those mangoes are sweet.
Interrogative: Whose car is this? Which way shall we go?
Possessive: This is my book. They sold their house.

4. Verb (ক্রিয়া)

A Verb is a word that expresses an action, occurrence, or state of being. যে শব্দ দ্বারা কোনো কাজ করা, হওয়া বা থাকা বোঝায়, তাকে Verb বলে।

Classification of Verbs

Verbs are mainly classified into two types: Finite Verb (সমাপিকা ক্রিয়া) and Non-finite Verb (অসমাপিকা ক্রিয়া).

A. Finite Verb (সমাপিকা ক্রিয়া): A verb that has a subject and shows tense. It completes the meaning of a sentence.

Principal/Main Verb: (go, eat, play, read, write, sleep, run, walk, talk, see, hear, think, learn, teach, etc.)
Auxiliary/Helping Verb: (am, is, are, was, were, be, being, been, have, has, had, do, does, did, shall, will, should, would, may, might, can, could, must, ought to, need, dare, used to).

Principal Verbs can be Transitive (সকর্মক - needs an object) or Intransitive (অকর্মক - does not need an object).

Transitive: He reads a book. I play football.
Intransitive: The baby sleeps. Birds fly.
Auxiliary: He is reading. They have finished the work.

B. Non-finite Verb (অসমাপিকা ক্রিয়া): A verb form that does not show tense and is not limited by the subject. বাক্যের অর্থ সম্পূর্ণ করে না।

1. Infinitive (টু + verb): (to go, to eat, to play). Example: He wants to go home.

2. Gerund (verb + ing, acts as a noun): (walking, swimming, reading). Example: Walking is a good exercise.

3. Participle (acts as an adjective):

  • Present Participle (verb + ing): A rolling stone gathers no moss.
  • Past Participle (verb 3rd form): This is a broken chair.
  • Perfect Participle (having + verb 3rd form): Having finished his work, he went to bed.

5. Adverb (ক্রিয়া বিশেষণ)

An Adverb is a word that modifies a verb, an adjective, or another adverb. এটি verb, adjective বা অন্য adverb-কে বিশেষায়িত করে।

Classification of Adverbs

1. Adverb of Manner (কিভাবে): Shows how an action is done. (slowly, quickly, bravely, beautifully, loudly, hard, fast, well).

2. Adverb of Place (কোথায়): Shows where an action is done. (here, there, everywhere, near, far, up, down, in, out, inside, outside, away).

3. Adverb of Time (কখন): Shows when an action is done. (now, then, today, yesterday, tomorrow, soon, late, early, always, never, before, after, daily, recently).

4. Adverb of Frequency (কতবার): Shows how often an action is done. (always, often, sometimes, usually, generally, never, ever, once, twice, again, frequently, seldom).

5. Adverb of Degree/Quantity (কি পরিমাণে): Shows how much or to what extent. (very, much, too, quite, almost, fully, enough, so, rather, partly).

6. Interrogative Adverb (প্রশ্নবাচক): Used to ask questions about time, place, manner, etc. (When, where, why, how).

Sentence Examples:

Manner: He walks slowly.
Place: Come here.
Time: I will go tomorrow.
Frequency: He always speaks the truth.
Degree: She is very beautiful.
Interrogative: When will you come?

6. Preposition (পদান্বয়ী অব্যয়)

A Preposition is a word placed before a noun or a pronoun to show its relation to some other word in the sentence. এটি Noun বা Pronoun-এর পূর্বে বসে বাক্যের অন্য শব্দের সাথে তার সম্পর্ক স্থাপন করে।

Common Prepositions:

about, above, across, after, against, among, around, at, before, behind, below, beneath, beside, besides, between, beyond, by, down, during, for, from, in, inside, into, near, of, off, on, onto, out, outside, over, since, through, throughout, to, toward, under, underneath, until, unto, up, upon, with, within, without.

Sentence Examples:

The book is on the table.
He is going to school.
The cat is under the chair.
I am fond of music.

Appropriate Prepositions (BCS için খুবই গুরুত্বপূর্ণ):

Abide by (মেনে চলা), Accused of (অভিযুক্ত), Addicted to (আসক্ত), Angry with (a person), Angry at (a thing), Aware of (সচেতন), Believe in (বিশ্বাস করা), Boast of (গর্ব করা), Capable of (সক্ষম), Complain against (বিরুদ্ধে অভিযোগ), Congratulate on (অভিনন্দন), Consist of (গঠিত), Die for (দেশের জন্য মরা), Die of (রোগে মরা), Die from (অতিরিক্ত কোনো কারণে মরা), Fond of (প্রিয়), Grateful to (a person), Grateful for (a thing), Ignorant of (অজ্ঞ), Jealous of (ঈর্ষান্বিত), Key to (সমাধান), Laugh at (উপহাস করা), Listen to (শোনা), Loyal to (অনুগত), Popular with (জনপ্রিয়), Prefer to (অধিকতর পছন্দ করা), Proud of (গর্বিত), Rely on (নির্ভর করা), Responsible for (দায়ী), Sure of (নিশ্চিত), Wait for (অপেক্ষা করা)।

7. Conjunction (সংযোজক অব্যয়)

A Conjunction is a word that joins words, phrases, or clauses. যে শব্দ দুটি শব্দ বা বাক্যকে যুক্ত করে, তাকে Conjunction বলে।

Classification of Conjunctions

1. Coordinating Conjunctions: Joins two elements of equal grammatical rank. (Mnemonic: FANBOYS - For, And, Nor, But, Or, Yet, So).

2. Subordinating Conjunctions: Joins a dependent (subordinate) clause to an independent (main) clause. (after, although, as, because, before, if, since, that, though, till, unless, until, when, where, while).

3. Correlative Conjunctions: Pairs of conjunctions that work together. (either...or, neither...nor, not only...but also, both...and, whether...or).

Sentence Examples:

Coordinating: Rahim and Karim are brothers. He is poor but honest.
Subordinating: Wait here until I come back. He could not come because he was ill.
Correlative: He is not only a fool but also a knave. You can have either tea or coffee.

8. Interjection (অনন্বয়ী অব্যয়)

An Interjection is a word or phrase that expresses a sudden feeling or emotion. এটি আকস্মিক আবেগ বা অনুভূতি প্রকাশ করে এবং এর শেষে সাধারণত একটি আশ্চর্যবোধক চিহ্ন (!) বসে।

Common Interjections:

For Joy (আনন্দ): Hurrah! Haha!
For Sorrow (দুঃখ): Alas! Ah!
For Surprise (বিস্ময়): Oh! What! Ha!
For Approval (সমর্থন): Bravo! Well done!
For Calling (সম্বোধন): Hello! Hey!
For Contempt (ঘৃণা): Fie! Pish! Pooh! Shame!

Sentence Examples:

Hurrah! We have won the game.
Alas! He is no more.
Bravo! You have done well.

Determiner

A Determiner is a word that introduces a noun. It comes before a noun and specifies the noun's reference. Modern grammar often treats determiners as a separate part of speech, while traditional grammar includes them under Adjectives. BCS পরীক্ষার জন্য Determiner গুলো চেনা খুব জরুরি।

Types of Determiners

1. Articles: a, an, the.

2. Demonstratives: this, that, these, those.

3. Possessives: my, your, his, her, its, our, their, whose.

4. Quantifiers: some, any, no, many, much, more, most, little, few, fewer, enough, several, all, both, each, every, either, neither.

5. Numbers: one, two, ten, hundred, first, second, etc.

Sentence Examples:

I have a pen. The boy is reading.
Give me that book.
This is my house.
He has many friends.
She has little knowledge about it.

BCS Questions & Answers

Q1: 'Honesty' is which kind of noun? [37th BCS]

Answer: Abstract Noun.

Q2: Which one is a common noun? (a) Salt (b) Army (c) Student (d) Honesty [36th BCS]

Answer: (c) Student.

Q3: What is the verb of the word 'ability'? [35th BCS]

Answer: Enable.

Q4: "He walks slowly." In this sentence, 'slowly' is a/an -

Answer: Adverb (of Manner).

Q5: Which of the following is a neutral gender? (a) King (b) Queen (c) Monarch (d) Drone

Answer: (c) Monarch (ruler).

Q6: "The jury found the prisoner guilty." Here 'jury' is a/an - [11th BCS]

Answer: Collective Noun.

Q7: A rolling stone gathers no moss. Here 'rolling' is a -

Answer: Participle (Present Participle acting as an adjective).
