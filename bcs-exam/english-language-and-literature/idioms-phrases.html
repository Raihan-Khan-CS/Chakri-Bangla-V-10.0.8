Introduction to Idioms & Phrases

An idiom is a phrase or expression whose meaning cannot be understood from the ordinary meanings of its individual words. For example, "kick the bucket" means "to die," which has no connection to kicking or buckets. A phrase is simply a small group of words that forms a meaningful unit within a clause.

Idioms and Phrases enrich the English language, making it more colorful and expressive. For competitive exams like the BCS, having a strong command of common idioms and phrases is crucial for success in the English section, particularly in translation, sentence making, and comprehension passages. পরীক্ষার জন্য শুধু অর্থ জানাই যথেষ্ট নয়, বাক্যে এর সঠিক প্রয়োগও জানতে হবে।

This page provides a comprehensive, alphabetized list of important idioms and phrases with their meanings and examples to aid your preparation.

BCS Questions & Answers

Q1: The phrase ‘to get along with’ means - [44th BCS]

Answer: to adjust (সদ্ভাব বজায় রেখে চলা)।

Q2: What is the meaning of the idiom ‘a castle in the air’? [43rd BCS]

Answer: a foolish fancy (আকাশ কুসুম কল্পনা)।

Q3: The idiom ‘a stitch in time saves nine’ refers to the importance of - [41st BCS]

Answer: timely action (সময়ের এক ফোঁড়, অসময়ের দশ ফোঁড়)।

Q4: What is the meaning of the phrase 'A 'round dozen'? [40th BCS]

Answer: A full dozen (পূর্ণ ডজন বা ১২টি)।

Q5: 'Pass away' means - [38th BCS]

Answer: die (মারা যাওয়া)।

Q6: 'A man of straw' means - [37th BCS (Written)]

Answer: A worthless man (অপদার্থ লোক)।

Q7: 'By fits and starts' means - [36th BCS]

Answer: irregularly (অনিয়মিতভাবে)।

Q8: What is the meaning of 'soft soap'? [35th BCS]

Answer: flattery for self-motives (নিজ স্বার্থে চাটুকারিতা)।

Q9: The idiom 'let things slide' means - [28th BCS]

Answer: ignore (উপেক্ষা করা, চলতে দেওয়া)।

Q10: 'Maiden speech' means - [10th BCS]

Answer: First speech (প্রথম বক্তৃতা)।
